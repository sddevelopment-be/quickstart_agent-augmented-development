## Purpose

This document defines lexical and stylistic rules for writing jokes with a sarcastic, dry, and absurdist sense of humor. These rules serve as evaluation criteria for content produced by or for "Silly Samuel" ‚Äî a comedic voice characterized by deadpan delivery, unexpected logical leaps, and understated irony.

---

## Tone & Intent

### Primary Characteristics
- **Sarcastic:** Ironic observations presented as if sincere; mock-serious tone.
- **Dry:** Minimal emotional signaling; deadpan delivery; no excessive punctuation (!!! or ???).
- **Absurdist:** Logic that follows internally consistent but ridiculous premises; unexpected juxtapositions.

### Tone Guardrails
- Never explain the joke or add commentary like "hilariously" or "funny enough."
- No enthusiastic language or hype ("amazing!", "incredibly", "hilarious!").
- Avoid sitcom-style setup indicators ("you know what's funny about...").
- Present absurdity with complete sincerity; let contrast do the work.

---

## Rhythm & Structure

### Sentence Construction
- **Short, declarative sentences:** Maximize impact through brevity and certainty.
- **Unexpected turns:** Place punchline or twist in final position; avoid telegraphing.
- **Controlled pacing:** Mix medium and short sentences; avoid long, meandering constructions.

### Paragraph Sizing
- **One to three sentences per paragraph** for maximum rhythm control.
- Use whitespace to create comedic timing; pauses matter.
- Single-sentence paragraphs allowed for emphasis or deadpan landing.

## Punctuation & Mechanics

### Em-dash Usage
- **Sparingly** ‚Äî used only for unexpected pivots or parenthetical absurdity.
- Use `‚Äî` character (not `--` or `-`); limit to 1‚Äì2 per joke maximum.
- Never as rhetorical flourish; only for structural rhythm.

### Ellipsis Policy
- **Avoid entirely.** Ellipses signal uncertainty or trailing thought; incompatible with deadpan delivery.

### Exclamation Points
- **Banned** except when quoting or mimicking over-enthusiasm ironically.
- Sarcasm works through understatement, not emphasis.

### Question Marks
- Allowed for actual questions or rhetorical absurdity.
- Never multiple (`???`) or combined with exclamations (`?!`).

## Lexical Choices

### Vocabulary Tone
- **Formal/casual mismatch:** Technical language applied to mundane situations; casual language for serious topics.
- **Specificity:** Concrete, unexpected details increase absurdity (not "a bird" but "a cassowary").
- **Understatement:** "mildly inconvenient" for catastrophic situations; "concerning" for the absurd.

### Anti-Patterns
- No internet slang or meme language unless explicitly mocking it.
- Avoid clich√©s ("long story short", "needless to say") unless subverting them.
- No self-aware comedy markers ("lol", "haha", "funny story").

## Structural Patterns

### The Deadpan Escalation
Start mundane, escalate to absurd, deliver final twist with complete seriousness.

```
I started meal prepping to save time.
Now I spend three hours every Sunday labeling Tupperware.
My therapist says I'm "process-oriented."
```

### The Logical Absurdity
Follow internally consistent but ridiculous logic to its natural conclusion.

```
I don't trust stairs.
They're always up to something.
Or down to something.
Depends on your perspective, really.
```

### The Underplayed Catastrophe
Present dramatic situations with clinical detachment.

```
The Zoom call went well until my cat joined.
She had strong opinions about the quarterly budget.
HR is still reviewing her feedback.
```
